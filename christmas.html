How to crochet christmas ornaments

Add a touch of handmade charm to your holiday decorations with these beautiful Crochet Christmas Baubles! This pattern guides you step by step to create classic round ornaments that are perfect for decorating your loved ones Christmas tree.

baubles are worked in the round and can be customized with your favorite colors, yarn textures, and decorative touches like ribbons, sequins, or embroidery. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced crocheter, this pattern is simple, fun, and quick to make — ideal for last-minute holiday crafting!

Start by making a magic ring and work six single crochets (sc) into the ring. Pull it tight to close the circle. Continue crocheting in rounds — do not join at the end of each round; instead, use a stitch marker to mark the first stitch of each new round.

In the second round, increase in each stitch around (12 stitches). In round three, 1 sc in next stitch, 2 sc in next — repeat around (18 stitches). Continue increasing in the same pattern, adding one more single crochet between increases in each round, until your circle measures about 4–5 cm (or the width you’d like your bauble to be).

Once you’ve reached the desired width, crochet several rounds without increasing, keeping the stitch count the same — this forms the middle part of the bauble. To shape the top, begin decreasing: sc in next 2 stitches, sc2tog (single crochet two together), and repeat around. In the next round, sc in next stitch, sc2tog — continue this pattern until the opening starts to close. Before it’s completely closed, add fiberfill stuffing to give the bauble a nice round shape. Don’t overfill — just enough to make it firm and smooth.

Finish decreasing until only a small hole remains, then cut the yarn, leaving a long tail. Use your yarn needle to weave the tail through the last few stitches and pull tight to close the hole completely. Secure the end and hide it inside the bauble.

To hang it, chain about 30 stitches with the same yarn, or attach a small ribbon loop to the top. You can also add a bow or embellishment for an extra festive touch. And that’s it — your crochet Christmas bauble is complete! Make a few in different colors and textures to create a beautiful, handmade set for your Christmas tree. These baubles also make lovely, eco-friendly gifts or can be used as decorations for holiday wreaths and garlands.
